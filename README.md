# Semantic Standards and Terminology Model (SSTM)

The Semantic Standards and Terminology Model (SSTM) defines the semantic foundation of the Semantic Standards Authority (SSA).  
It establishes authoritative terminology, semantic constraints, and meaning structures used across all SSA modules and documents.

## Purpose
To provide a unified semantic framework that ensures consistent terminology, stable meaning, and semantic lineage throughout SSA.

## Authority
SSTM derives its authority from the Master Authoritative Artifact (MAA) located in `ssa-root`.  
All SSA standards, protocols, and documents must align with SSTM.

## Scope
SSTM governs:
- terminology definitions  
- semantic constraints  
- meaning structures  
- semantic lineage and consistency  
- the semantic boundaries of SSA materials  

## Structure
SSTM consists of:
- authoritative terminology definitions  
- semantic rules and constraints  
- meaning relationships between terms  
- semantic lineage mappings  
- updates that preserve semantic integrity  

## Principles
SSTM is based on:
- semantic clarity  
- non‑ambiguity  
- minimalism  
- non‑derivative meaning structures  
- strict alignment with MAA  

## Usage
All SSA modules must:
- use terminology defined in SSTM  
- avoid introducing new terms without SSTM alignment  
- maintain consistent meaning across documents  
- prevent semantic drift or interpretive expansion  

## Authorship
SSTM is authored solely by Renatas Vaičiūnas.  
No derivative works may alter or reinterpret SSTM terminology or semantic structures.

## Status
SSTM is the authoritative semantic foundation of SSA.  
All SSA materials must comply with this model.
